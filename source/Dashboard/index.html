Universe Categories
The Inspection Intel coverage universe (22 companies) is divided into five categories by market-cap weight. The first three roll up into the Inspection-11 Index; the last two form the Flow & MOS-11 Index. The doughnut shows each category’s share of total market capitalization.
NDT Services
Pure-play NDT and inspection companies. Includes Mistras Group, Team Inc, Acuren Group, Oceaneering International, and Expro Group. These firms derive the majority of revenue from non-destructive testing, inspection services, or subsea asset integrity.
Global NDT
Large-cap international TIC (Testing, Inspection, Certification) conglomerates. Includes Bureau Veritas, Intertek Group, Comet Group, and SGS SA. These companies operate NDT divisions within broader multi-service platforms spanning quality assurance, certification, and regulatory compliance worldwide.
NDT Adjacent
Companies with significant exposure to inspection markets but whose primary business extends beyond traditional NDT. Includes Transcat (calibration services) and Thermon Group (process heating and monitoring). These provide complementary products and services to the core NDT ecosystem.
Flow Control
Valves, actuators, and engineered flow products serving process industries. Includes Flowserve, Rotork, IMI plc, Spirax Group, Weir Group, and Cactus Inc. Adjacent peer set used as a read-across for industrial capex cycles.
Mech. & On-Site Services
Pipeline, infrastructure, and industrial-services peers. Includes Matrix Service, Primoris, MasTec, Clean Harbors, and Forum Energy Technologies. Public proxies for the broader on-site services peer set.

How Sentiment Scoring Works
Each earnings call transcript is analyzed using a rule-based NLP engine with an NDT-industry-tuned lexicon of 60+ positive and 60+ negative terms.
Scoring Formula
Score = (positive word count − negative word count) per transcript. Higher scores indicate more optimistic management tone. Scores typically range from −10 to +40.
Score Ranges
> 20: Very bullish   10–20: Positive   0–10: Neutral   < 0: Bearish
Guidance Detection
The engine scans for guidance-related phrases (e.g., “raised outlook,” “lowered guidance,” “reaffirmed targets”) and classifies direction as Raised, Maintained, or Lowered.
Data Source
Based on the most recent earnings call transcript per company. Rankings show executive-only sentiment (CEO, CFO, COO speakers) to filter out analyst questions.
